History of Boba

How Taiwan started a worldwide obsession.

Boba tea was invented in Taichung, Taiwan in the 1980s. Two teahouses—Hanlin Tea Room and Chun Shui Tang—both claim to have created it. The original version had black tea, milk, syrup, and freshly made tapioca pearls.

By the 2000s, boba spread across Asia, then the U.S., becoming especially popular with teens and young adults. Today, you can find hundreds of variations, including cheese foam boba, popping pearls, and fruit jellies.
